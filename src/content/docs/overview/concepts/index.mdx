These articles split **Lambda Managed Instances (LMI)** into single-topic pages: where workloads run, how fleets scale, isolation and roles, sandbox behaviour, what publishing activates, and how you connect, observe, and respect limits. Use them to reason about design before you touch procedural sections.

**Suggested reading order (matches the sidebar):** [Placement & capacity](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/overview/concepts/capacity-providers/) → [Elasticity & CPU](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/overview/concepts/scaling/) → [Tenancy & isolation](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/overview/concepts/security/) → [IAM roles](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/overview/concepts/operator-role/) → [Concurrency & runtimes](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/overview/concepts/execution-environment/) → [Publishing & activation](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/overview/concepts/version-publishing/) → [Networking](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/overview/concepts/networking/) → [Monitoring](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/overview/concepts/monitoring/) → [Quotas](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/overview/concepts/quotas/). For procedures, continue to **[Walkthrough](/aws-lambda-managed-instance-walkthrough/walkthrough/)** after fundamentals.

For **quotas, API fields, and region-specific** behaviour, use [Lambda Managed Instances](https://docs.aws.amazon.com/lambda/latest/dg/lambda-managed-instances.html) as the product reference.
